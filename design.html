Propulsion

Our propulsion system can be likened to that of an airplane with one key difference, we do not use any fuel. It is 100% renewable and zero emissions. It heavily relies on the use of rechargeable batteries as its main source of power. The pod has a front mounted compressor, which scoops all the air it encounters and redirects it behind the capsule. Before the air exits the back of our pod, it is expanded with a nozzle. This expansion process creates the thrust we need as well as allows the pod to move through the tube with little to no friction at high speeds, where air density causes an increase in friction.

Levitation

Our pod design has individual passive magnets paired with electromagnets that are strategically positioned on top of the pod. These electromagnets allow individual fine control of the repulsion and attraction of the magnets. The pod therefore hangs from the top of the tube without the use of any external aids. Other systems rely on existent Maglev technology. It consists of levitating on top of a rail. Apart from requiring magnets along the entire length of the pod, the rail increases the cost of the infrastructure by an order of magnitude.

Structure

The structure and design of the Hyperloop pod is heavily constricted by the tube throught which it travels. Therefore the dimensions of the tube affect large aspects of the design. To occupy the maximum possible volume the pod is shaped like the tube. Its length is calculated so that it is approximately as long as the foundations holding the tube upright. This is done to attenuate the vibrations caused by the pod moving at extremely high speeds inside the tube. The pod is also made out of space-grade materials like those used in the aeronautical industry. This is because the conditions in the tube are very similar to those experienced by spacecraft.

Sub-Systems

There are two very important sub-systems that help the pod function correctly: the first is the navigation system. Because of the tube's own properties, it blocks all electromagnetic waves that could be used to communicate with the pod or obtain GPS signals. The pod therefore has a very robust navigation system consisting of multiple sensors that combined help the pod know exactly where it is. This includes sensors that detect strips on the inside of the tube. The second important sub-system is the communication system. For the exact same reasons stated before, communicating with a levitating pod is difficult. As a result the pod has a state-of-the-art system of high-energy lasers, which not only allow it to know where it is but also to communicate at light speed.
